Raila Amolo Odinga

The man who fought tirelessly for Democracy

    Here is a timeline for Raila Odinga's life:

  • 1945 - Raila Amolo Odinga was born on the 7th January 1945
  • 1971 - founded the Standard Processing Equipment
  • 1974 - Odinga was appointed group standards manager of the Kenya Bureau of Standards.
  • 1982 - Odinga was arrested and charged with treason after being accused of being among the masterminds of the 1982 coup.
  • 1988 - He was arrested again after being released in the same year for his pro-democracy and human rights agitation at a time when the country continued to descend deep into the throes of poor governance and the despotism of a single-party rule.
  • 1992 - Odinga returned to Kenya and joined the Forum for the Restoration of Democracy(FORD), a movement t oagitate for the return of multi-party democracy in Kenya. Raila had fled to Norway in 1991 following attempts to assasinate him.
  • 1992 - The General ELections were held FORD split into FORD-Kenya and FORD-Asili. Odinga became Ford-Kenya's Deputy Director od ELections and won the Langata Constituency parliamentary seat previously held by Philip Leakey of KANU. Odinga became the second father of multiparty democracy in Kenya after Kenneth Matiba.
  • 1997 - In his first bid for the presidency in the 1997 General Election, Odinga finished third after President Moi, the incumbent, and Democratic Party candidate Mwai Kibaki. He however retained his position as the Langata MP
